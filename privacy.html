OneShot Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how OneShot ("we," "our," "us") collects, uses, and protects personal information when you use the OneShot app and related services. OneShot is built for high school athletes — many of whom are minors — so this policy is written to be readable by athletes and their parents or guardians alike.

1. Who uses OneShot

  • Athletes — high school athletes (age 13+) building a recruiting profile: performance stats, highlight videos, academics, and personal brand.
  • Coaches — verified college coaching staff discovering and evaluating athletes.

Every piece of profile information in OneShot exists because a user entered it. We do not buy data about you or scrape it from other services.

3. How we use information

  • Provide the service: profiles, scores, leaderboards, feeds, chat.
  • Compute derived metrics (e.g., percentile scores against athletes of the same sport, position, and grade).
  • Facilitate discovery: make athlete profiles visible to verified coaches.
  • Send notifications you control in Settings.
  • Keep the platform safe: moderation, fraud prevention, enforcing our Terms and Compliance Policy.
  • Meet legal obligations.

We do not sell personal information. We do not serve third-party targeted advertising.

4. What other users can see

  • Visible to coaches and other users: your athletic profile, public highlight videos, scores and leaderboard placements, academics you add, and your personal-brand content.
  • Restricted by you: videos marked "Friends" or "Only Me" are enforced server-side; only the audiences you choose can view them.
  • Never visible to other users: your phone number and private contact details, your notification settings, your saved lists, and your private messages (visible only to conversation participants).

5. Minors' privacy

No children under 13. OneShot is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect their data (consistent with COPPA). Sign-up requires a date of birth; ages under 13 are refused. If we learn we hold data on a child under 13, we delete it promptly — write to privacy@oneshot.app.

Users 13–17 must have a parent or legal guardian's permission to use OneShot. A parent or guardian may:

  • review the child's personal information,
  • request corrections,
  • request deletion of the account and its content,
  • withdraw consent at any time,

by contacting privacy@oneshot.app with proof of guardianship.

Extra protections for minors:

  • No sale of minors' data; no targeted advertising to minors.
  • Coaches never see private contact details; all coach–athlete communication happens in-app, with blocking and reporting built in, subject to our NCAA-aligned Compliance Policy.
  • California residents under 18 may request removal of content they posted (Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 22581). Deletion controls are built into the app, and we honor written requests.

6. Sharing

We share information only with:

  • Service providers that host and operate the platform (e.g., Supabase for database, authentication, and media storage), bound by data-processing agreements.
  • Legal authorities when required by law or to protect safety.
  • A successor entity in a merger or acquisition, with notice to you.

7. Your rights

Depending on your location (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws), you may have the right to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict processing of your data, and to withdraw consent. In-app: Settings → Account covers most of these, including full account deletion. Otherwise: privacy@oneshot.app. We do not discriminate against users who exercise privacy rights.

8. Retention and deletion

  • Account data lives as long as your account does.
  • Deleting your account removes your profile, performance data, videos, messages, and personal content. Deletion is available directly in the app (Settings → Account → Delete Account).
  • We may retain limited records where the law requires it (e.g., safety reports, legal holds).

9. Security

Data is protected in transit (TLS) and at rest, access is controlled through row-level security so users can only read what they are authorized to see, and private videos are served through short-lived signed URLs.

10. International transfers

Data is processed in the United States. Where required, we use approved safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) for international transfers.

11. Changes

We will post material changes here and notify you in the app. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance.
